One of the hard things about having an amazing idea is that the result might not end up being what you want it to be. That was, unfortunately, the case for my project. This art piece it the culmination of my procrastination. Memes help me when I want a laugh, but they are alluring, constantly begging for my attention. While I DID want to do something cool, like a text-based choice game, I realized that it was not going to be possible with the time frame given (and my inability to stay focused on writing the frickn' story). So, I chose to do something inspired by the college student's worst nightmare.

The art piece is a gif filled with links around it. While simple, you will first be greeted by a very loud sound that might sound like music, but alas, it is the cries of the hours of sleep washed away. The humor here just represents my grasp at trying to come up with a replacement project when there was less than 12 hours before the project was due. It is quick, but shallow. All the self-deprecation throughout the work as well as this statement represents my knowledge that this is pretty much all my fault at this point. I could have started earlier. I could have chosen a better project. But instead, I chose a difficult idea that could not be well done in 2 weeks by a novice/intermediate web-programmer, if at all.

Above all, I hope that this art serves a purpose. While yes, I would prefer if no one takes the time to analyze how much time actually went into this, I would like for anyone who sees it to see the lesson I learned. I don’t want to get this stressed about a project then be extremely ashamed of the result like this one again. On top of that, I also wanted to somewhat point out how annoying this meme culture can be sometimes while still being very comical. But that is not the real purpose, just something I came up with hours before the due date…
